## About

The Backwoods adds a dark new dimension to Minecraft. Built from oak and shadow, it's a foggy world where ash drifts through the air.

To enter, build a portal from oak planks and light it with a Flint and Charcoal.

Inside, you'll find Splinters, hostile, humanoid creatures made of oak plank that relentlessly pursue you. They mine through walls, build bridges, and climb to reach you. But stare them down and they freeze completely. Look away for even a second and they're moving again.

---

## The Splinter -- social anxiety / being watched

The Splinter only moves when you're not looking. This shows the feeling that people are judging you, talking about you, or closing in on you the moment you're not paying attention. When you face them directly, when you engage, they freeze and stay silent. They can't hurt you if you just keep watching. But you can't watch forever. The moment you look away, they're moving again. That's exhausting hypervigilance.

---

## The Hollow -- avoidant attachment / trust issues

Hollows are drawn to you but unpredictable. They appear next to you uninvited, like someone who gets too close too fast, and there's a chance that proximity hurts you. You didn't ask for the closeness, and sometimes it punishes you anyway. They vanish the moment you push back.

---

## The Backwoods Itself

A world made entirely of wood, something man-made, artificial, constructed, but overgrown and abandoned. It looks like somewhere people used to be, but isn't anymore. Dense fog. You're always exposed. The ash falling is like the residue of something that burned out long ago.

---

## Dead Trees vs Living Trees

The dead trees in Wood Plains and Deep Backwoods are what's left after connection dies. The Thicket has living trees, it's the hope that things could grow again, but it's still in The Backwoods. Growth doesn't mean safety.

---

## Features

- **The Backwoods Dimension** -- dense fog, no caves, ash particle effects
- **Wood Plains Biome** -- oak plank terrain with dead oak trees
- **Deep Backwoods Biome** -- spruce terrain with towering dead spruce trees
- **The Thicket Biome** -- oak terrain with living trees
- **Splinter Mob** -- stare-to-freeze mechanic, mines through blocks, builds bridges and climbs to reach the player
- **Log Splinter Mob** -- same AI as the Splinter, oak log variant
- **Hollow Mob** -- teleports next to you in caves, vanishes when struck
- **Flint and Charcoal** -- one-time use item to light the portal

---

## How to Enter

1. Build a portal frame out of oak planks (same shape as a Nether portal)
2. Craft Flint and Charcoal using 1 flint + 1 charcoal (same recipe layout as Flint and Steel)
3. Light the portal frame with your Flint and Charcoal
4. Step through

The portal destroys itself upon entry.

---

## The Splinter

Splinters are the primary inhabitants of The Backwoods. They are relentless and intelligent.

- **Stare-to-freeze** -- if you look directly at a Splinter, it cannot move
- **Block mining** -- Splinters mine through walls to reach you, with speed based on block hardness
- **Building** -- Splinters place oak planks to climb and bridge toward you
- **30 HP** -- they hit hard and don't give up
- **Drops** -- 0 to 2 oak planks on death

---

## The Hollow

Hollows haunt The Backwoods differently from Splinters. They do not chase. They appear.

- **Cave stalking** -- teleports next to you randomly while you are underground
- **Proximity** -- appears beside you and stares
- **Vanishes on hit** -- strikes against the Hollow do no damage; it disappears into smoke
- **Unpredictable** -- there is a chance it leaves you with darkness and a sound you can't place
